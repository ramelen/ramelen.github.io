Contrasting Opinions of the Importance of Animals

There are many opinions on the importance of animals. Two such opinions are detailed in a book, “Grey Eyes”, and an Atlantic article. They describe two very different situations, where one reverses animals and the other thinks of them as a means to a more important end. I will outline several differences between the excerpts within each paragraph.

In “Grey Eyes”, Blue Elk Man remarks about how sad he was when he killed his first large animal, an elk. He says: “I was thinking of my family when I made the kill, but I still felt guilty afterwards.” The only benefits of killing the buffalo were the physical resources it could provide, and there was a cultural hate towards killing in general. This made waste the worst of both worlds. He felt terrible after a minor death, and it clearly affects his decisions and his worldview.

In the article by the Atlantic, the Phippen writes that “[t]he Army wasn't in the business of guiding hunting trips for soft skinned Wall Streeters, but it was in the business of controlling the Native Americans in the area, and that meant killing buffalo”. The killing of buffalo was only good, because the benefit of driving away the indiginous people was the only thing they saw, not the waste or possible endangerment of buffalo. Large amounts of killing didn’t affect the people at all, as it was masked by the benefits.

After Blue Elk Man speaks, Painted Turtle Man speaks about the reason that they never waste any part of the animal: “We must always honour our brother animals. We need them for food and furs. That is why we make every effort to see that the entire animal finds a purpose and that nothing is ever wasted”. A lot of thought is put into the animals, because they have constructed a whole story around the animals because they care about them a lot. In the article, a colonel is thought to have said: “Kill every buffalo you can! Every buffalo dead is an Indian gone”. This suggests that the killing is just a means to an end, and that there is no immediate value to the killing of the buffalo. Not much thought is put into the buffalo, all of it has been put into the eradication of the indigenous people.

Painted Turtle Man explains that “[w]hen one of our animal brothers presents itself to a hunter it is sacrificing itself for the good of the Nehiyawak”. Each animal has a purpose and an individual. They have a lot of importance assigned to them, because if they were not there, the people would not survive. This is a relationship where the people are very conservative when it comes to the animals, and the animals are plentiful and easy to get.

In the article a person killed 30 buffalo in one hunting trip and had not assigned any importance to the animals, and the buffalo had been abstracted into another form, driving away the indiginous people. The buffalo had no individuality and were just a number in the mind of a hunter. It wasn’t about the buffalo, so the value was thrown away in turn for political victory.
