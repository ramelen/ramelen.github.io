I have a lot of projects. Sometimes they go somewhere, and sometimes they don’t. Usually they don’t. And I often find myself trying to figure out which ones have the highest chance of going somewhere. Usually my project ideas have two dimensions when I categorize them; Coolness, and Difficulty. Coolness is defined by how much I want to work on the project and how useful it would be if I finished it. Very cool things are basically guaranteed to make it on my list of possible future projects, and less cool things I quickly forget about. Difficulty is about how much time and effort I would need to put into the project in order to complete it, with hard projects having a lower chance of succeeding. Then if the coolness and difficulty are within certain bounds, I try the project. After I’ve completed the project, the coolness transforms into pride. This pride is what my object centers around. What type of project generates the most pride in my worldview?

You could represent my pride as being roughly proportional to the product of the coolness and the difficulty of the project. That is to say, they compound each other. Projects which are very hard and not very cool, like say, school, have an amount of pride, though much less pride than the difficulty justifies. Conversely, something very cool that I whack out in an hour, say, a custom app color theme (I’m such a nerd) I often end up taking for granted and it doesn’t generate a lot of pride. The most prideful project is one that is very difficult, and also very cool. So some candidates for hard projects might be; Starting a business; Running a marathon; Writing a book; Getting a masters degree; etc. Things that require hundreds or thousands of hours of time, thousands of dollars, and a fair amount of skill. Things that I find cool usually are; Intellectually complex and stimulating; Very elegant at their core; and able to be used in a large number of ways. This is why I like math and programming so much. They are the most complex and practical yet elegant systems you could possibly imagine.

Which brings me to the ideal project. What is difficult, respected, elegant, intellectually complex, and useful? Why, that’s a book! Books are a culmination of thousands of an author's thoughts, carefully constructed into an object of knowledge. They’ve been around for millennia and have served a myriad of purposes, always adapting to their own specific needs and uses. For me, books are the perfect project, a beautiful balance of complexity and difficulty.

The whole reason I think about this is because it is deeply connected with my relationship with knowledge. I view knowledge as a great thing in and of itself, and so much of my life is built around that fact. I spend a lot of my free time learning, with what’s left going towards creating knowledge and experiences to put out into the world. Most of my not free time is spent at school, developing skills and learning even more. I have no interest in being an athlete, nor becoming an actor, but I consider any afternoon spent in front of a blackboard as a day well spent. This is my relationship with knowledge, as it is shapes, and is shaped by, my worldview.
